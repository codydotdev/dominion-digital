## [Role: Why It Matters]

[Narrative]
Search, display, and retargeting serve different roles in a paid campaign. Search captures buyers who are actively looking right now. Display builds awareness with a cold audience that hasn't searched yet. Retargeting brings back visitors who showed interest but didn't convert. Running all three without a plan for how they interact means spending without a system.

Strategy defines what each campaign is supposed to accomplish, how much budget it earns, and how the data from each campaign informs the others. A business with a clear cost-per-acquisition target can look at any campaign and know whether it's working. A business without one can only guess.

[Punchline]
Without a target, every result looks acceptable. A clear cost-per-acquisition makes the math honest.

## [Role: FAQ]

**How is PPC strategy different from Google Ads management?**
Strategy is the plan: architecture, platforms, funnel structure, budget logic, and CPA targets. Management is the ongoing execution: bids, copy testing, optimization, and reporting. Dominion.Digital offers both. Some clients come for strategy first and then hand off management. Others bring us in for both from the start.

**Do you build strategies for platforms besides Google?**
Yes. While Google Search is the primary platform for most Kansas City service businesses, paid social and display play a role in full-funnel campaigns. Dominion.Digital includes platform recommendations as part of every strategy engagement so budget goes where the economics support it.

**What results should I expect in the first 90 days?**
The first 30 days are setup and launch. Days 31–60 produce the first round of real performance data. Days 61–90 are the first optimization cycle. By day 90, a well-built campaign has clean attribution data, a baseline cost-per-lead, and a clear picture of which elements to scale. Most Kansas City businesses see their first qualified leads within the first two weeks of campaigns going live.
