## [Role: Opening]

[Narrative]
Most businesses running Google Ads or Meta campaigns make the same mistake. They spend real money driving traffic and then send that traffic to their homepage. The homepage has navigation, multiple CTAs, and a dozen reasons to click away. The visitor came looking for one thing and leaves without finding it.

A landing page is a single-purpose page built around one offer, one audience, and one action. No nav links to pull attention away. No competing CTAs. Just the message that matches the ad and a clear path to convert. Landing page design for paid campaigns produces a higher conversion rate than homepage traffic because it removes every distraction standing between the visitor and the form.

Dominion.Digital builds landing pages for Kansas City businesses running paid campaigns who want their ad spend to produce results instead of just clicks.

[Punchline]
A landing page is the only part of your marketing system where the sole job is to convert.

## [Role: FAQ]

**How is a landing page different from a page on my website?**
A landing page has one job: convert the visitor who arrived from a specific ad or campaign. It has no main navigation, no links to other parts of your site, and no competing calls to action. A regular website page serves multiple audiences and multiple goals. Landing pages work better for paid traffic because they stay focused on the one thing the visitor came to do.

**Do I need a separate landing page for every campaign?**
For best results, yes. Each campaign or ad group that targets a distinct audience or offer should have its own page with matching language. Running traffic from a Google Search ad about commercial roofing to the same page as a Meta retargeting ad about residential windows reduces performance on both. Dominion.Digital can scope a set of pages that covers your active campaigns without building more than you need.

**Can the landing page connect to my CRM or email system?**
Yes. The form on your landing page is connected to your CRM, email platform, or both as part of the build. When a lead submits, they enter your system automatically. No manual imports, no missed contacts.
