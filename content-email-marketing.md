## [Role: What This Service Includes]

[Rapid Fire Benefits]
- Automated Sequences

- Deliverability Setup
- Engagement Tracking
- CRM Integration

[Scope / Inclusion List]
Every email marketing engagement includes:

- **Welcome and nurture sequences:** automated email series that go out to new leads when they first enter your list, delivering the right message at the right interval to move them toward a decision
- **Broadcast campaigns:** one-time sends to your full list or a segment for promotions, seasonal offers, announcements, or re-engagement of cold contacts
- **List segmentation:** your list is organized by lead source, engagement level, service interest, or any criteria that matters so the right message goes to the right group
- **Deliverability configuration:** SPF, DKIM, and DMARC records set up correctly so your emails reach inboxes instead of spam folders
- **Engagement tracking:** open rates, click rates, and conversion events monitored so you can see what's working and what needs adjustment
- **CRM integration:** email activity synced to your CRM so your sales team has full visibility into every contact's engagement history before they reach out

## [Role: FAQ]

**What email platform do you use?**
Dominion.Digital works with the platform you're already on. If you have an existing account in Klaviyo, Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign, HubSpot, GoHighLevel, or similar, the work gets done there. If you don't have a platform yet, Dominion.Digital recommends one based on your list size, CRM, and how much automation you need.

**How often should I email my list?**
For most Kansas City service businesses, once or twice a month is enough for broadcast sends. Automated sequences run on their own schedule based on when each contact entered the list. The right frequency depends on your audience and your offer. Dominion.Digital maps the cadence before anything is sent so you're not guessing.

**Can you work with a list I've already built?**
Yes. If you have an existing list, Dominion.Digital starts with an audit to assess list health, segment what's there, and identify inactive contacts that need a re-engagement sequence before regular sends begin. A list that hasn't been emailed in over six months needs to be warmed up correctly to protect your sender reputation.
